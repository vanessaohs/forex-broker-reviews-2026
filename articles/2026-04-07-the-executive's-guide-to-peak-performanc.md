# The Executive's Guide to Peak Performance and Longevity

High-performing executives are increasingly treating their bodies like their most valuable asset. Here's the playbook the top 1% are following.

## The Problem: Success at the Cost of Health

The typical C-suite executive works 60+ hours per week, sleeps poorly, exercises inconsistently, and relies on caffeine and willpower. By 50, the cumulative damage is measurable: elevated cortisol, insulin resistance, declining testosterone, and accelerated biological aging.

## The Solution: Systematic Health Optimisation

The same systematic thinking that builds companies can optimise health — if you have the right data and the right team.

### Step 1: Comprehensive Baseline

Before any intervention, you need data. A proper executive health assessment includes:
- Full blood panel (100+ markers)
- Hormonal profiling
- Genetic testing (APOE, MTHFR, etc.)
- VO2 max and metabolic testing
- DEXA body composition scan
- Coronary calcium score

### Step 2: Protocol Design

Based on your data, a personalised protocol might include:
- Hormone optimisation (testosterone, thyroid, DHEA)
- NAD+ IV therapy (weekly or bi-weekly)
- Targeted supplementation based on deficiencies
- Peptide therapy for recovery and inflammation
- GLP-1 agonists for metabolic health if indicated

### Step 3: Ongoing Monitoring

Quarterly blood work, wearable data review, and protocol adjustments. The best outcomes come from continuous optimisation — not one-off interventions.

## The ROI of Executive Health

Consider this: a CEO earning $1M+ per year who loses 10 productive years to preventable disease has lost $10M+ in earnings alone — not counting quality of life, family time, and legacy.

Investing $20-50K annually in comprehensive health optimisation is arguably the highest-ROI investment any executive can make.
